Introducing: Armand Zorn

Some information about my motivation, career and personal life

Welcome!

On 26 September 2021, I was elected to the German Bundestag, representing the constituency of Frankfurt am Main I. I was born in Yaoundé, Cameroon and lived there until the seventh grade. In 2000, I moved to Germany without speaking any German.

My Motivation

Fighting for a just and sustainable future

The world is changing: our society is undergoing profound structural changes. Globalisation, the climate crisis, digitisation and the coronavirus pandemic are confronting us with major challenges. To master these challenges, we need a capable state that acts decisively and seizes opportunities. I want to play an active part in shaping this change.

Background and Experience

I grew up in modest circumstances, and education has always been the key to my opportunities. I studied Politics and Public Administration, European Affairs, International Economics and Business Law in Halle (Saale), Paris, Chongqing, Konstanz and Bologna.

Professionally, I have worked at the Federal Ministry of Finance, the German Bundestag, the French National Assembly and the Representation of the European Union in Hong Kong. Before being elected, I was a consultant on digital transformation, risk management and regulatory compliance.

Career Path

  • 1988 – Born and raised in Yaoundé, Cameroon
  • 2000 – Moved to Germany at the age of 12
  • 2007 – High school graduation in Halle (Saale)
  • 2007–2012 – B.A. in Political Science and Humanities, Halle-Wittenberg
  • 2009–2010 – Exchange year at Sciences Po Paris
  • 2013–2014 – M.A. Politics & Public Administration, Konstanz
  • 2014–2015 – Studies in Bologna & Chongqing
  • 2015–2021 – Consultant in digital transformation
  • 2021 – Elected to the Bundestag
  • 2022 – Deputy speaker on finance for the SPD parliamentary group

My Committees

I am a member in the Committee on Finance and on Digital Affairs and a deputy member in the Committee on European Union Affairs.

With the exception of budgetary policy, the Finance Committee’s remit is identical with that of the Federal Ministry of Finance. It deliberates on draft bills, motions, reports and EU items. It usually leads deliberations on financial market regulation, the customs system and tax policy.

The Committee on Digital Affairs is dedicated to Internet policy issues, including the expansion of digital infrastructure. It discusses aspects of digitalisation and connectivity and takes important decisions concerning the digital transformation.

The Committee on European Union Affairs is the central forum for the Bundestag’s participation in European matters. It deals with European integration, institutional topics, strategy documents and enlargement issues. With 40 members, it is one of the Bundestag’s largest committees.
